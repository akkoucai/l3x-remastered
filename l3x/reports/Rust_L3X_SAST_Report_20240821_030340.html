Summary

Summary of Static Application Security Testing (SAST) Findings

High-Severity Vulnerabilities:

  1. Unauthorized Modifications:
    • Ensure the expected signer account has signed (ID: VULN008).
    • Validate that state accounts are writable (ID: VULN009).
    • Verify the owner of state accounts to prevent fake data injection (ID: VULN010).
    • These issues were found on multiple lines (35, 36, 37, 38, 39, 40, 84, 85, 86, 87, 88, 118, 119, 120, 121, 122, 123).

Medium-Severity Vulnerabilities:

  1. Boundary Checks:

    • Accessing arrays or vectors without boundary checks can lead to panics or buffer overflows (ID: Rust-Solana007).
    • Found on several lines (22, 23, 59, 90, 132).

  2. Error Handling:

    • Unwrapping a Result or Option without error handling can lead to panic (ID: RUST002).
    • Overuse of unwrap() or expect() can lead to issues; prefer match or if let (IDs: RUST024, Rust-Solana002).
    • These were observed on lines (69, 103, 145).

Low-Severity Vulnerabilities:

  1. Performance Issues:
    • Cloning large data structures can cause performance issues due to excessive memory use (ID: Rust-Solana008).
    • Found on various lines (58, 71, 72, 73, 74, 105, 106, 107, 108, 147, 148, 149, 150).

Conclusion:

The SAST findings highlight several critical and medium-level vulnerabilities mainly relating to inadequate validation of signer accounts, insufficient boundary checks on arrays and vectors, and improper error handling. Performance issues were also flagged due to unnecessary cloning of large data structures. Addressing these vulnerabilities is essential to enhance the security and efficiency of the application.
