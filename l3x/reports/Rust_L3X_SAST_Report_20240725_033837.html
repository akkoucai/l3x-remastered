Summary

The Static Application Security Testing (SAST) tool has identified a series of security vulnerabilities and issues in the Rust codebase for the Sharky project. Below is a summary of the findings categorized by severity: ### High Severity Issues: 1. **Arithmetic Operations (Overflow/Underflow)**: Numerous instances across multiple files, particularly in `update_nft_list.rs`, `offer_loan.rs`, and `repay_loan_v3.rs`, where arithmetic operations might overflow or underflow without proper checks. Notable lines include 17, 90, and many within `repay_loan_v3.rs` and `extend_loan_v3.rs`. 2. **Unauthorized Account Modifications**: There are multiple occurrences in `take_loan_v3_compressed.rs`, `foreclose_loan_v3.rs`, and `repay_loan_v3.rs` where the expected signer account is not validated, leading to potential unauthorized modifications (lines 106, 167, 295). 3. **Checking Writable State Accounts**: Missing checks on the writability of state accounts can lead to unauthorized modifications, repeated across several files (lines in the `take_loan_v3.rs`, `repay_loan_v3.rs`, and `extend_loan_v3.rs`). ### Medium Severity Issues: 1. **Array or Vector Boundary Checks**: Multiple files report accessing arrays or vectors without boundary checks, which could result in panics or buffer overflows. Key files include `foreclose_loan_v3_compressed.rs`, `repay_loan_v3_compressed.rs`, and many files concerning `extend_loan_v3.rs` (e.g., lines 102, 131, 217). 2. **Unwrapping Results/Options**: Several instances of unwrapping a `Result` or `Option` without adequate error handling, which could cause panics. Noteworthy line occurrences can be found across the `repay_loan_v3.rs` and `extend_loan_v3.rs`. 3. **Excessive Memory Use**: Cloning large data structures without caution might lead to performance issues, found in `util.rs` and `extend_loan_v3.rs`. ### Low Severity Issues: - **Performance Issues**: Less critical warnings regarding overuse of `unwrap()` or `expect()`, suggesting a shift to safer error handling mechanisms like `match` or `if let`. ### Overall Observation: The findings highlight critical areas within the Sharky project that require immediate attention, particularly around arithmetic operations and authorization checks. Addressing these issues is essential to enhance the security and stability of the application.
