Summary of Findings from SAST Tool

High Severity Issues:

  1. Overflow/Underflow Risks (ID: RUST012, RUST021):

    • Found at multiple lines (59, 65, 66, 69, 82, 106, 124, 143, 46, 66, 79, 92, 97, 99, 108).
    • Arithmetic operations are performed without checks for overflow or underflow.

  2. Authorization and State Modifications Risks (IDs: VULN008, VULN009, VULN010):

    • Found at lines (40, 41, 42, 81, 82, 83, 110, 111, 112).
    • Ensure expected signer accounts have signed, state accounts are writable, and verify owner to prevent unauthorized modifications.

Medium Severity Issues:

  1. Unwrapping Results/Options (IDs: RUST002, RUST024, Rust-Solana002):

    • Found at multiple lines (97, 114, 133, 44, 74, 103).
    • Unwrapping without proper error handling may lead to panics; prefer using match or if let.

  2. Array/Vector Boundary Checks (ID: Rust-Solana007):

    • Found at multiple lines (17, 18, 19, 34, 38, 62).
    • Accessing arrays or vectors without boundary checks can lead to panics or buffer overflows.

  3. Cloning Large Data Structures (ID: Rust-Solana008):

    • Found at line 61.
    • Potential performance issues due to excessive memory use.

Recommendations:

  • Implement checks for arithmetic operations to handle potential overflow or underflow.
  • Enhance error handling by replacing unwrap() and expect() with appropriate error handling techniques.
  • Ensure proper signing and validation of account states to strengthen security.
  • Add boundary checks when accessing arrays or vectors to avoid potential panics or crashes.
